Project Documentation

This thesis investigates optimization techniques for the Consistent Vehicle Routing Problem with a Heterogeneous Fleet (HConVRP) and introduces a Variable Neighborhood Descent (VND) algorithm.

This problem integrates fleet heterogeneity and service consistency, focusing on minimizing costs while maintaining reliable service delivery over multiple periods. The research highlights the importance of consistent service in sectors like healthcare and grocery delivery, where customer retention and satisfaction are crucial.

The VND algorithm, designed to solve HConVRP, systematically explores different neighborhood structures in routes, such as Change Vehicle Chain, Swap, Relocation, and 2-opt. It aims to improve initial solutions while balancing costs and consistency constraints, offering a flexible and efficient framework for logistics optimization.

The computational results show that VND performs competitively, particularly in smaller or less complex scenarios, compared to existing frameworks like Hierarchical Tabu Search (HTS).

This custom web application was developed to enhance the usability of the HConVRP solver. It facilitates solution generation, evaluation, and solution comparison, providing users with a comprehensive tool for managing and analyzing routing solutions.
